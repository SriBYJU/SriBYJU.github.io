What the Pros Think

Ben Johns: Widely regarded as the #1 player in the world, Ben emphasizes tactical precision and shot variety as the keys to controlling the kitchen line. He focuses on changing pace and placement to keep opponents off balance, which reflects years of PPA Tour experience.
Anna Leigh Waters: Known as one of the top female professionals, Anna Leigh stresses consistent placement and awareness. She believes that staying alert to opponent positioning is as important as technique, especially at high-speed rallies.
Federico Staksrud: An elite international competitor, Federico focuses on angled dinks that force opponents out of position. He uses subtle body and paddle movements to disguise shots, creating openings to attack.
Tyson McGuffin: A top-ranked PPA pro, Tyson highlights the importance of footwork and anticipation. His insight is that positioning often decides high-pressure points more than raw power.
Collin Johns: Collin, a consistent PPA doubles champion, emphasizes strategic resets and smart placement to control the pace of rallies. He often uses soft touches to force opponents into errors while maintaining offensive potential.
Gabriel Tardio: Known for his precision and consistency, Gabriel believes offensive control at the kitchen line is crucial. His philosophy is that well-placed dinks, combined with patience, force mistakes and create scoring chances.
