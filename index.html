Advanced Dinking Techniques Overview

Dinking is one of the most important skills in high-level pickleball. Mastering both offensive and defensive dinking strategies allows a player to control the kitchen line, maintain rallies, and set up scoring opportunities.

  • Soft Hands & Paddle Angle: Maintain control while keeping the ball low and unattackable.
  • Disguising Shots: Hide your offensive intent until the last moment.
  • Resetting: Recover from tough positions to stay aggressive at the net.
  • Footwork & Positioning: Stay balanced and prepared for all returns.

Defensive Dinking

Defensive dinking focuses on keeping the ball in play while waiting for the opponent to make a mistake. It requires patience, precision, and consistency. Top players emphasize that good defensive dinking allows you to reset the point and maintain control at the kitchen line.

  • Use a loose grip to absorb speed and control ball height.
  • Angle the paddle slightly upward to keep the ball low over the net.
  • Maintain proper distance from the net and anticipate opponent movement (USA Pickleball Coaching Committee, 2023).
  • Mix in cross-court and straight dinks to force movement.
Pro Tip: Watch professional doubles matches on the PPA Tour to see how players defend with soft dinks while forcing errors.

Offensive Dinking

Offensive dinking is about creating scoring opportunities. It uses deception, speed changes, and placement to put pressure on opponents. Advanced players like those on the PPA Tour combine offensive dinking with strategic movement to set up put-away shots.

  • Push dinks to the opponent’s feet to limit their options.
  • Use sudden directional changes to force weak returns (Pickleball360 Analysis, 2024).
  • Disguise your intentions until the last moment to create openings.
  • Combine offensive dinks with aggressive positioning at the net.

Spin & Footwork

Spin and footwork are key to controlling dinking exchanges. Proper spin allows you to manipulate the ball trajectory, while good footwork ensures you can recover and stay ready.

  • Topspin: Makes the ball drop faster, useful for offensive dinks.
  • Backspin: Slows the ball down, keeps it low, and gives defensive control.
  • Side Spin: Can create angles and push opponents out of position.
  • Footwork: Stay on your toes, move with small, quick steps, and always anticipate the next ball.
Pro Tip: Top players like Tyson McGuffin and Catherine Parenteau use footwork to maintain balance and apply precise spin consistently.
